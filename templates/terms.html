02 · The service

Sohana is a platform that digitises community-based savings systems — commonly known as ROSCAs, Tontines, Njangis, Esusu, Chamas, or Tandas. It also includes pool savings, fundraising campaigns, and a multi-currency wallet.

At launch, Sohana will operate as an agent of a licensed payment institution. We are not a bank. Wallet balances will not be bank deposits and are not covered by government deposit insurance schemes. During beta, no real funds are held; the platform is for demonstration and onboarding only.

What we provide

  • Coordination tools for group savings — contributions, payouts, member management, audit trails
  • A multi-currency wallet system supporting EUR, GBP, USD, CAD, XAF, GHC, NGN, ZAR (at launch)
  • The Njangi Credit Score (NCS) — a behavioural credential built from your platform participation
  • Hanatag handles — universal addresses for cross-border payments

What we do not provide

  • Financial, tax, legal, or investment advice — Sohana is a coordination tool, not an adviser
  • A bank account — your balance is not a bank deposit
  • A guarantee that other members will fulfil their obligations within a community-savings circle

06 · Groups & community

Sohana facilitates group-based financial activity (ROSCAs, Pools, Campaigns). When you join a group, you participate in a community agreement with other members.

Our role

We provide the technical infrastructure: ledger, audit trail, contribution tracking, payout orchestration, and the NCS. We do not arbitrate disputes between members about money owed outside what the platform records.

Your responsibility

  • Choose your circle wisely. Only join groups with people you trust. We display NCS scores to help you assess.
  • Honour your contribution schedule. Missed contributions affect your NCS and other members' payouts.
  • Communicate with your group. Most disputes can be resolved by talking before they reach us.
  • If something goes wrong, raise a complaint via our complaints process. We review platform data and activity logs to support fair resolution.

Organiser responsibilities

If you create a circle, pool, or campaign, you are the organiser. You set rules within the platform's framework. You must apply them fairly. You cannot use organiser tools to disadvantage members for arbitrary reasons.
